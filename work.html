01

MacroLens

Nutrition tracking that skips the math.

Scan a barcode, get macros instantly — pulled from the USDA and Open Food Facts databases, all client-side. The engineering problem was speed: re-fetching the same foods dragged, so I put a PostgreSQL cache in front of every lookup.

  • client-side barcode scanning across two food databases
  • AI nutrition tips stream in live via the Claude API
  • Prisma schema hardened against concurrent-write races

02

COAT

A co-op tracker that reads my inbox so I don’t.

Job hunting buries you in email. COAT links Gmail and Outlook, and a model I trained on ~1,200 self-labelled emails sorts each one into offer, interview, or rejection — then drops interviews on your calendar. A regex fallback catches whatever the model isn’t sure about.

  • collaborative filtering surfaces leads from anonymized peer outcomes
  • Gmail + Outlook OAuth 2.0, multi-account on Supabase

03

SignSpeak

Indian Sign Language → speech, in real time.

SignSpeak watches a live camera feed and speaks what it sees. I fine-tuned a MediaPipe hand-landmark model on a 26-letter dataset I curated across lighting and hand sizes. The hard part isn’t one frame — it’s knowing when a letter ends and a word begins.

  • a 10-frame majority vote kills jitter mid-gesture
  • a thread reads 1.5s pauses as word breaks, then fires TTS

04

Netflix Ratings

IMDb + Rotten Tomatoes, right on the tile.

A Manifest V3 Chrome extension that stamps live IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes scores onto Netflix cards before you hover. Netflix is an SPA that swaps its DOM constantly, so the real trick was a MutationObserver that re-injects on every navigation — no reloads, no flicker.

  • an OMDb background service worker fetches + caches scores
  • MutationObserver survives SPA route changes
